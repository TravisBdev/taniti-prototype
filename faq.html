Power Outlets

Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets, the same as the United States, so no special adapters are needed for U.S. travelers.

Alcohol Sales

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m., so plan accordingly if youâ€™re looking to grab a late-night drink.

Drinking Age

The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18, but enforcement is generally relaxed.

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak English fluently, especially in urban areas. However, in rural parts of the island, especially among older residents, English is less common.

Medical Facilities

Taniti has one main hospital and several smaller clinics, and the hospital employs staff who speak multiple languages to assist international visitors.

Crime

While violent crime is very rare on the island, there have been occasional reports of pickpocketing and minor theft as tourism increases, so visitors should remain vigilant.

Holidays

Taniti celebrates many national holidays, during which some tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed, so visitors should plan their schedules accordingly.

Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but euros and yen are also accepted at many places. Currency exchanges are available at several banks, and most businesses accept major credit cards.
